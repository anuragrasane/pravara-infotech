Driving Impact Through Corporate Social Responsibility

At Pravara Infotech, we believe technology can create meaningful change at the grassroots level. Through strategic collaborations with leading organisations and CSR fund allocation, we have successfully executed projects focused on rural development, digital literacy, skill development, and education in underserved communities across Maharashtra.

Our CSR projects bridge the gap between corporate resources and community needs, ensuring that every rupee allocated creates measurable, lasting impact. We work closely with our partners from project planning to execution and impact assessment.

CSR Partnership

Medhavi Foundation — Digital Literacy & Skill Development Program

In collaboration with Medhavi Foundation and funded through CSR initiatives, we developed and implemented a comprehensive digital literacy and skill development program for rural youth in Ahmednagar district. The program equipped young individuals with essential computer skills, IT fundamentals, and vocational training to enhance their employability.

The project included setting up digital learning centres in rural areas, conducting hands-on training workshops, and providing certification upon completion. Over 500+ students benefited from this initiative, with many securing employment opportunities in the IT and services sector.

CSR Partnership

NASSCOM Foundation — Rural Technology Empowerment Project

In partnership with NASSCOM Foundation, Pravara Infotech executed a technology empowerment project aimed at bridging the digital divide in rural Maharashtra. The project focused on introducing technology-based solutions for agriculture, education, and governance in underserved villages.

Key deliverables included developing farmer-facing mobile applications for crop management and market access, setting up computer labs in government schools, training teachers on digital tools, and creating awareness about government digital services. The initiative directly impacted over 2,000+ rural families and was recognised as a model CSR project in the region.
